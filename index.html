What is Making Tax Digital (MTD)?

Making Tax Digital (MTD) for Income Tax is HMRC's programme to digitise the UK tax system. From 6 April 2026, sole traders and landlords with gross income over £50,000 must keep digital records using HMRC-approved software and submit a quarterly summary of income and expenses directly to HMRC. It replaces the single annual self-assessment return for those in scope. Genius Tax handles this entirely on your behalf.

Who needs to comply with MTD from April 2026?

MTD ITSA is mandatory from 6 April 2026 for self-employed individuals and landlords whose gross income (turnover — not profit) from self-employment and/or property exceeds £50,000 in the 2024/25 tax year. PAYE and umbrella income does not count toward the threshold. If you're unsure, sign up and we'll confirm your position for free.

What penalties will I face if I miss an MTD deadline?

HMRC uses a points-based penalty system for MTD. Every missed quarterly deadline earns a penalty point. Once you reach the threshold (4 points for quarterly filers), HMRC issues a £200 financial penalty — plus further £200 penalties for each subsequent missed submission. Unlike the old SA system, the penalty clock starts from 6 April 2026, not the end of the tax year. Getting compliant early avoids any risk.

Is the threshold really dropping to £30K in 2027?

Yes. The MTD ITSA threshold is scheduled to drop to £30,000 in April 2027, bringing significantly more self-employed people and landlords into scope. If your income is between £30K and £50K today, you'll likely be in scope next year. Signing up to the Standard or MTD plan now means a seamless transition without any scramble later.
